Introduction to Coronary Artery Disease

Coronary Artery Disease (CAD) is a condition characterized by the narrowing or blockage of the coronary arteries, which supply blood to the heart muscle. This restriction is primarily caused by atherosclerosis, a buildup of fatty deposits, cholesterol, and other substances on the artery walls. Contributing factors include genetic predisposition, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, and lifestyle choices such as poor diet and lack of exercise. Early detection through screening and effective management strategies, including lifestyle changes, medications, and procedures like angioplasty or coronary artery bypass surgery, are crucial for reducing the risk of heart attacks and improving overall cardiovascular health.

HISTORY OF THE TREATMENT OF CORONARY ARTERY DISEASE

The first coronary artery bypass graft (CABG) in 1960 and the subsequent development of percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) revolutionized the treatment of coronary artery disease (CAD) (Doenst et al, 2022). Treatment options for CAD vary based on disease severity and patient symptoms, including medications, surgical procedures, and lifestyle changes. Medications such as aspirin, statins, beta-blockers, and calcium channel blockers lower cholesterol, manage blood pressure, and prevent blood clots. When medications are insufficient, surgical options like angioplasty and stenting, CABG, and, in severe cases, heart transplants are considered. Angioplasty opens blocked arteries, sometimes with stent placement, while CABG uses a healthy vessel to bypass blockages. Lifestyle changes, including a heart-healthy diet, regular exercise, quitting smoking, and managing stress, are vital for improving cardiovascular health (Bansal et al, 2023). Treatment choice depends on blockage location, severity, overall health, and other medical conditions.

HOW THE APP WORKS

The web application facilitates the early detection and classification of coronary artery disease (CAD) using machine learning algorithms. It features two primary forms for user interaction. In the first form, users enter patient details such as age, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels. Upon clicking the "Predict" button, the application analyzes the data using a machine learning model to determine if the patient has CAD, displaying the result instantly. The second form helps determine the number of major vessels colored by fluoroscopy. Users provide additional health information, and the application predicts the number of affected vessels using a specialized model. The result is displayed immediately, aiding in further clinical decision-making. Additionally, the web app educates users on the causes, prevention, and treatment options for CAD. This app aims to enhance CAD diagnosis and management by providing quick, accurate predictions, supporting early intervention, and improving patient outcomes while also increasing awareness and knowledge about the disease.
